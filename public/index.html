About Kirehe Adventist Technical Secondary School

We are committed to providing a holistic education that integrates faith, learning, and service, preparing students for both career success and meaningful lives.

Our History & Vision

Founded in 2004, KATSS was established to address the critical need for skilled technical professionals in the Kirehe region and Rwanda. Our vision is to be the leading technical and vocational education provider, recognized for academic rigor, innovative spirit, and strong Adventist values.

Mission, Vision & Values

Mission

To empower students with career-ready technical skills and instill Christian principles for responsible citizenship and lifelong learning.

Vision

To produce globally competitive, innovative, and ethically grounded technical professionals who contribute positively to national development.

Core Values

  • Integrity
  • Excellence
  • Service
  • Innovation

Academic Overview & Programs

Nurturing the Future of Technology and Leadership

At Kirehe Adventist Technical Secondary School (KATSS), we are committed to providing a holistic and technology-focused education. Our curriculum is designed to balance academic rigor with practical technical skills, preparing students for university education and immediate professional impact in Rwanda's growing economy.

---

Our Academic Pathways

We offer comprehensive secondary education across three key levels to ensure a solid foundation for every student.

Ordinary Level (S1 - S3)

Focuses on establishing a strong foundation in core sciences, humanities, and languages. This stage is crucial for developing critical thinking and discipline before specialization.

Advanced Level (S4 - S6)

Students select combinations of professsional accounting for university entrance examinations and higher education.

Technical Vocational Training (TVET)

Dedicated programs where students gain certification in highly sought-after trades. This includes hands-on training for immediate employability after graduation.

---

Specialized Technical Offerings (TVET)

Our six technical programs provide job-ready skills tailored to Rwanda's economic demand.

Software Development

Focuses on programming, web design, mobile app development, and database management, preparing future developers.

Accounting

Training in financial record-keeping, basic bookkeeping, tax compliance, and commercial practices using modern software.

Multimedia and Production

Covers graphic design, video editing, photography, and audio production for media and communications careers.

Building Construction

Practical training in masonry, carpentry, site supervision, drafting, and sustainable building techniques.

Tourism

Focuses on hospitality management, tour guiding, travel agency operations, and promoting Rwanda's cultural heritage.

Automobile Technology

Hands-on experience in engine repair, electronic diagnostics, vehicle maintenance, and modern automotive systems.

Admissions Requirements & Steps

1. Overview

Kirehe Adventist Technical Secondary School (KATSS) welcomes prospective students who are driven, ambitious, and ready to embrace a hands-on learning environment. Our admission process is designed to select candidates who demonstrate strong academic potential and a commitment to technical excellence and moral values. We encourage all interested students to apply promptly, as spaces in our highly-regarded technical programs are limited.

Contact Admissions Office

2. Available Programs (Trade Options)

We offer specialized programs recognized nationally for their quality and relevance to the modern job market. Students select one of the following trades:

  • Software Development
  • Building Construction
  • Accounting
  • Automobile Technology
  • Tourism
  • Multimedia Production

3. Application Requirements

To be considered for admission, applicants must submit the following documents and meet the stated qualifications:

  • Primary School Certificate (P6 or equivalent): For entry into Secondary 1 (S1).
  • Junior Secondary Certificate (S3 or equivalent): For entry into Senior Secondary 4 (S4) Technical Programs.
  • Academic Transcripts/Report Cards:Copies of the last two years of academic records.
  • Two recent passport-size photographs.
  • Copy of National ID or Passport/Birth Certificate.
  • Application Fee Payment Slip: Proof of payment of the non-refundable application fee.
  • Entrance Exam:Applicants must pass the mandatory entrance examination administered by the school or national education board (as applicable).
